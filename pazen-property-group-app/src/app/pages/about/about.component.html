Lease Reviews - Financial & legal

Lease disputes often arise between landlord and tenant. We can assist to solve this dispute.

In most instances where a property is leased to a tenant, there will be a lease document, the lease forms the basis of the contract between the Landlord and the Tenant.

The concept of a lease has been around for centuries, and sometimes they are still written in very old fashioned difficult to understand language. Sometimes, they are just written by draftsmen who has not clearly phrased the clauses within the lease. It may also be badly written and does not cover the essentials of the agreement.

Whatever the case, it often results in a lease that needs formal interpretation, and a trained eye to advise on what the lease terms actually mean. Through our experience and expertise we are able to assist in reviewing your lease, either in full, or with a specific query.

A few examples of the questions you may have are:

• Am I charging/ being charged the correct amounts?
• What alterations can a tenant do without consent?
• How much rental should I be charging or paying, is this market related?
• What items of expenditure can be recovered as a service charge?
• Who is responsible for maintenance of the common areas?

We are able to assist with all of these questions and more, on a fixed fee basis.

Property Due Dilligence

Value in commercial properties is determined inversely to the risk posed to the income of the property. Multi tenanted properties in both Commercial and Retail are the most complicated.

The function of due diligence is to verify and understand the risks involved. Usually the list of Due Diligence items should be disclosed during the acquisition negotiations and included in the purchase agreement.

Every document relating to the operation of the building must be checked. Including leases, addendums, mortgages, title deeds, compliance certificates, insurance policies, maintenance contracts licences etc. Our team will provide the entire due diligence in the time stipulated in the agreement.
